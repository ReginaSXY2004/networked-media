🏆 My Skating Secret

When I was in kindergarten, I was on a roller-skating team—and even made it on TV! A children's channel filmed our performance, and I was the lead skater, guiding everyone through intricate obstacles. Some tricks required sharp turns, while others involved ducking under low bars at full speed.

That experience became my proudest achievement—because it meant that when I first stepped onto an ice rink, I could glide effortlessly, even spinning around like a pro. Or so I thought.

Fast forward to college. I was in Seattle, excited to relive my glory days on the ice. I stepped onto the rink with confidence, ready to impress. Twenty minutes later, I had fallen four times. One particularly spectacular fall sent me sliding half a meter across the ice, flat on my back. Concerned strangers gathered around me, asking, “Are you OK?” I laughed awkwardly, scrambled to my feet, and clung desperately to the rink’s edge for the rest of the session.

Turns out, my former skating skills had vanished into thin air. My childhood triumphs was just a distant illusion.
